Wind Loading Analysis of High Rise Building

Wind Loading Analysis of High Rise Building

Research Project | University of Toronto

Project Overview

  • Advanced numerical modeling of wind effects on high-rise structures
  • Computational fluid dynamics analysis using OpenFOAM and ANSYS Fluent
  • Validation against wind tunnel testing data
  • Compared with WT tests, validated CFD methods to be applied in design codes (Wind section of ASCE 7-16) and studied the effect of plan dimensions on wind loads for high-rise

Technical Scope

  • Development of CFD models for various building geometries
  • Implementation of turbulence models and boundary conditions
  • Mesh Generation analysis and optimization
  • Post-processing and visualization of results

Key Achievements

  • Successfully validated numerical results against experimental data
  • Optimized computational efficiency through advanced meshing techniques
  • Developed recommendations for wind load calculations
  • Attended conferences, drafted original manuscripts and conference proceedings, and published on peer-reviewed Journal

Tools & Technologies

  • OpenFOAM for CFD simulations
  • ANSYS Fluent for validation
  • Python for data analysis and visualization
  • ParaView for flow visualization
